Understanding Your Kidneys

Your kidneys are remarkable organs that work silently around the clock to keep your body in balance. This article will provide a clear, simple overview of what your kidneys are, where they are located, and the vital functions they perform every single day.

What are Kidneys and Where are They?

Humans typically have two kidneys, each about the size of a fist. They are bean-shaped organs located on either side of your spine, just below the rib cage. You can imagine them situated towards your back, which is why some kidney-related pain can be felt in the flank area.

How Do Kidneys Work? The Filtering Process

Think of your kidneys as a highly advanced purification system. Your entire blood supply passes through them several times a day. Inside each kidney are about a million tiny filtering units called nephrons. Each nephron contains a filter, called the glomerulus, and a tubule. The glomerulus filters your blood, and the tubule returns needed substances to your blood and removes wastes.

The process is simple yet elegant:

  • Blood enters the kidney's nephrons.
  • Waste products (like urea and creatinine) and excess water are filtered out in the glomerulus.
  • Essential minerals and most of the water are reabsorbed back into the blood through the tubule.
  • The remaining waste products and excess water become urine.
  • Urine flows from the kidneys to the bladder through tubes called ureters.

Key Takeaway

Your kidneys are vital, complex organs responsible for much more than just producing urine. Understanding their function is the first step towards protecting them.
