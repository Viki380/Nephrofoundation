Support & Resources

Being diagnosed with and living with a chronic condition like kidney disease can be a life-changing experience. It's normal to feel overwhelmed, anxious, or uncertain. Building a strong support system and knowing where to find reliable information are essential parts of managing your health and well-being.

Building Your Personal Support System

Emotional and practical support can come from many places. It's important to identify and lean on your network, including family, friends, and your healthcare team. Connecting with other patients in support groups can also be incredibly powerful.

Trusted Online Resources

The internet is full of information, but not all of it is accurate. It's crucial to rely on reputable sources like the National Kidney Foundation (NKF) and the National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases (NIDDK).

Key Takeaway

Managing a chronic illness is a marathon, not a sprint. Building a strong support network and arming yourself with knowledge from trusted sources are two of the most important things you can do to live well with kidney disease.
